Fettuccine Carbonara

Description:

Fettuccine carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish known for its creamy, velvety sauce made from eggs, cheese, and pancetta. The wide, ribbon-like strands of fettuccine are tossed in a rich mixture of beaten eggs and grated Pecorino Romano or Parmesan cheese, which, when combined with the heat of the pasta, creates a silky sauce that clings to each noodle. Crisp, salty pancetta or guanciale is cooked until golden and then mixed with the pasta, adding a savory depth and crunch to the dish. Black pepper is often added for a hint of spice, balancing the richness of the sauce. Fettuccine carbonara is beloved for its indulgent yet simple combination of flavors, offering a satisfying blend of creamy, salty, and savory notes.

Ingredients:

  • 5 teaspoons olive oil
  • 4 shallots, diced
  • 1 pound bacon, cut into strips
  • 1 large onion, cut into thin strips
  • 1 clove garlic, chopped
  • 1 (16 ounce) package dry fettuccine pasta
  • ¾ cup shredded Parmesan cheese
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • 3 egg yolks
  • salt and pepper to taste

Steps:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large heavy saucepan over medium heat. Sauté shallots until softened. Stir in bacon and onion; cook and stir until bacon is evenly browned. Stir in garlic when bacon is about half done. Remove from heat.
  2. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Add pasta and cook for 8 to 10 minutes or until al dente. Drain pasta, then return it to the pot.
  3. Whisk Parmesan, cream, and egg yolks together in a medium bowl. Pour bacon mixture over pasta; stir in cream mixture. Season with salt and pepper.
